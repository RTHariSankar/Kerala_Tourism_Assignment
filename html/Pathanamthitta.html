Pathanamthitta


Pathanamthitta is a small district in the southern Indian state of Kerala, known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and religious significance. The district is surrounded by the Western Ghats, and its numerous waterfalls, forests, and wildlife sanctuaries make it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts. The district also has a rich cultural heritage, with many traditional art forms, music, and dance performances, which provide a glimpse into Kerala's vibrant culture.


Pathanamthitta is also known for its religious significance, with several famous temples, churches, and mosques located in the district. The Sabarimala Temple, located in Pathanamthitta, is one of the most popular pilgrimage destinations in South India, attracting millions of devotees every year. The district also hosts the Aranmula Boat Race, one of the oldest and most famous boat races in Kerala, which attracts tourists from all over the world.


Overall, Pathanamthitta is a must-visit destination for tourists seeking a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and religious significance. With its diverse attractions and warm hospitality, Pathanamthitta offers an unforgettable experience for tourists visiting Kerala.

Places of Interest


GAVI

Gavi is a picturesque eco-tourism destination located in the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala, India, known for its pristine forests, scenic lakes, and abundant wildlife. The destination offers a range of outdoor activities for tourists, including trekking, camping, bird watching, and jeep safaris, making it a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts. Visitors can also explore the region's rich biodiversity and learn about the conservation efforts undertaken by the local communities. With its unspoiled natural beauty and eco-friendly tourism practices, Gavi offers a unique and unforgettable experience for tourists seeking a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

ARANMULA

Aranmula is a quaint village located in the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala, India, known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful backwaters, and traditional handicrafts. The village is famous for the Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple, a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, which attracts pilgrims from all over the world. The village is also known for its traditional metal mirrors, known as Aranmula Kannadi, which are handmade by skilled artisans using a secret alloy of metals. Visitors can also explore the village's beautiful backwaters by boat and enjoy the serene and peaceful ambiance of the village. With its unique blend of culture and natural beauty, Aranmula offers a refreshing and memorable experience for tourists visiting Kerala.

PERUNTHENARUVI WATERFALLS

Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls is a breathtakingly beautiful waterfall located in the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala, India, known for its scenic beauty and serene ambiance. The waterfall cascades down from a height of over 100 feet and offers a spectacular sight for visitors who come to witness its beauty. Visitors can take a refreshing dip in the cool waters of the waterfall and enjoy the serene and tranquil atmosphere of the surrounding forests. The region is also popular among trekking enthusiasts who come to explore the nearby hills and forests. With its unspoiled natural beauty and peaceful ambiance, Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls offers a refreshing and rejuvenating experience for tourists visiting Kerala.

KADAMMANITTA

Kadammanitta is a beautiful village located in the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala, India, known for its vibrant culture and scenic beauty. The village is famous for the Kadammanitta Padayani, a unique ritualistic art form performed during the annual Onam festival. The festival attracts thousands of tourists from all over the world who come to witness the colorful and vibrant performances. Visitors can also explore the village's beautiful landscapes, including lush green forests, sparkling streams, and picturesque hills. With its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, Kadammanitta offers a unique and unforgettable experience for tourists seeking a glimpse into Kerala's vibrant culture.

SABARIMALA

Sabarimala is a famous pilgrimage center located in the Periyar Tiger Reserve of the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala, India. The shrine is dedicated to Lord Ayyappa and attracts millions of pilgrims from all over the world every year, especially during the annual Mandala-Makaravilakku season. Visitors need to trek through the dense forests to reach the temple, which is situated on a hilltop. The temple's serene and peaceful atmosphere and stunning natural surroundings make the journey a memorable experience. The region is also popular among trekkers and nature lovers who come to explore the beautiful forests and hills. With its unique blend of spirituality and natural beauty, Sabarimala offers a one-of-a-kind experience for tourists visiting Kerala.

KODUMON CHILANTHIYAMBALAM

Kodumon Chilanthiyambalam is a historic temple located in the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala, India, known for its unique architecture and spiritual significance. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is believed to have been built during the 8th century AD. Visitors can explore the temple's ancient architecture, which features intricately carved stone pillars and beautiful murals depicting mythological scenes. The temple is also home to several ancient scriptures and artifacts that provide a glimpse into Kerala's rich cultural heritage. With its unique blend of history, architecture, and spirituality, Kodumon Chilanthiyambalam offers a fascinating and enriching experience for tourists visiting Kerala.
