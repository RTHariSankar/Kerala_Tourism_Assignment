Palakkad


Palakkad, also known as Palghat, is a small town in Kerala that is surrounded by the lush green Western Ghats. It is home to some of the most beautiful natural sights, historical monuments, and cultural heritage. The town is renowned for its serene ambiance and tranquil surroundings, which makes it a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. One of the major attractions of Palakkad is the Palakkad Fort, built by Haider Ali in the 18th century. This fort is a significant landmark that holds great historical significance. It has been well-maintained, and visitors can explore its well-preserved architecture, intricate carvings, and old relics that tell the story of the town's past.


Another attraction is the Silent Valley National Park, one of the most remarkable ecological reserves in India. The park boasts a diverse range of flora and fauna, including some of the endangered species like the lion-tailed macaque and the Nilgiri langur. The park is also home to several waterfalls and rivers, making it a perfect spot for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Palakkad is also known for its unique and vibrant festivals that reflect the town's rich culture and heritage. One of the most famous festivals is the Thrissur Pooram, held in the nearby town of Thrissur. It is a grand celebration that involves a procession of beautifully decorated elephants, traditional music, and fireworks. The town's own festivals, such as the Kalpathy Ratholsavam and the Malampuzha Garden Festival, are equally exciting and attract visitors from all over the world. Palakkad is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. Whether you're interested in exploring ancient forts, hiking through lush forests, or experiencing vibrant festivals, Palakkad has something for everyone.

Places of Interest


SILENT VALLEY NATIONAL PARK

Silent Valley National Park is a biodiversity hotspot located in the Palakkad district of Kerala. It is a popular destination for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts, known for its rare and endemic species of flora and fauna. The park is home to several endangered species like the Lion-tailed macaque and the Nilgiri langur. Visitors can take guided nature walks and trekking tours to explore the pristine forest and admire its scenic beauty. The park's diverse topography, including the rolling hills and the river Kunthi, also make it an ideal spot for adventure activities like camping and rafting.

NELLIYAMPATHY

Nelliyampathy is a picturesque hill station located in the Palakkad district of Kerala. Known for its serene natural beauty, lush green forests, and tea and coffee plantations, it is a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The misty hills offer breathtaking views of the surroundings, and visitors can indulge in trekking, camping, and other outdoor activities. The Pothundy Dam, Seethargundu Viewpoint, and the Kesavan Para Waterfall are some of the popular attractions in Nelliyampathy. The region is also known for its pleasant climate throughout the year, making it an ideal getaway destination.

PALAKKAD FORT

Palakkad Fort, also known as Tipu's Fort, is a historical monument that attracts tourists with its unique architectural style and rich cultural heritage. Built-in the 18th century by Hyder Ali of Mysore, this fort is a classic example of the fusion of traditional and modern styles. The fort walls, bastions, and gateways still stand tall and showcase the military prowess of the past. Today, the fort is a popular tourist attraction, where visitors can explore the ancient structure, view exhibits, and enjoy the tranquil surroundings. The fort's historic charm, along with the lush greenery and peaceful ambiance, makes it a must-visit destination in Palakkad.
