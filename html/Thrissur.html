Thrissur


Thrissur has a rich and glorious history that dates back to ancient times. The city was once ruled by the Chera dynasty, which was followed by the rise of the Zamorins of Calicut. Later, it was a part of the Cochin Kingdom, which played a significant role in the development of the city. During the colonial era, Thrissur came under the British Raj and was a part of the Madras Presidency until the formation of Kerala in 1956.


Thrissur is known for its vibrant culture and traditions that have been preserved over the years. The city is famous for its rich art and cultural heritage, which is reflected in its numerous temples, churches, and mosques. The Thrissur Pooram festival, celebrated in the Vadakkunnathan Temple, is one of the most spectacular and colorful festivals in Kerala. The city is also famous for its classical art forms, such as Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Theyyam.


Thrissur is a popular tourist destination in Kerala, known for its ancient temples, cultural festivals, and natural attractions. The Vadakkunnathan Temple, one of the oldest temples in Kerala, is a must-visit for anyone interested in architecture and heritage. The Athirapally Waterfalls, located about 70 km from the city, is a stunning natural attraction that attracts visitors from all over the world. Thrissur is also home to several museums and art galleries, including the Kerala Sahitya Akademi, the Kerala Lalithakala Akademi, and the Archaeological Museum.

Places of Interest


ATHIRAPPILLY WATERFALL

Athirappilly Waterfall is a popular tourist destination located in the Thrissur district of Kerala. It is a breathtakingly beautiful waterfall cascading from a height of 80 feet amidst lush green forests and rocks. The natural splendor and scenic beauty of Athirappilly Waterfall make it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

SAKTHAN PALACE

Sakthan Palace, located in Thrissur, Kerala, is a majestic palace built in the traditional Kerala style of architecture. It was the residence of the rulers of the Kingdom of Cochin and is now a popular tourist destination. Visitors can explore the museum inside the palace which houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the rich history and culture of Kerala. The palace is a must-visit for history and architecture enthusiasts and offers a glimpse into the state's rich cultural heritage.

PUNNATHUR KOTTA

Punnathur Kotta is a famous elephant sanctuary located in Guruvayur, Kerala. It is the largest elephant park in the world and is home to more than 60 elephants. Visitors can see the majestic creatures up close and learn about their daily lives. The sanctuary is a popular tourist destination for those interested in wildlife and nature. It's a unique experience that allows visitors to connect with these gentle giants in a peaceful and natural setting.
