Idukki


Idukki is a stunning hill station in Kerala, located in the Western Ghats mountain range. It is known for its picturesque beauty, tea plantations, and wildlife sanctuaries. The hill station is a perfect getaway for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The major tourist attractions in Idukki include the Idukki Arch Dam, the highest arch dam in Asia, and the Kalvari Mount, a popular spot for trekking and hiking.


Idukki is also home to several wildlife sanctuaries like the Periyar National Park and the Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary. The Periyar National Park is famous for its rich biodiversity and is home to various species of animals like elephants, tigers, leopards, and many more. The Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary is also home to various species of animals like elephants, sambars, bison, and a variety of birds.


Another major attraction in Idukki is the hill station of Munnar, known for its tea plantations and misty mountains. Visitors can explore the tea gardens, taste different types of tea, and take a tour of the tea factory. The town also offers opportunities for trekking, hiking, and camping in the wilderness. In conclusion, Idukki is a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, offering a perfect blend of serene beauty, wildlife, and adventure. With its rich biodiversity, stunning landscapes, and beautiful tea gardens, Idukki is a must-visit destination in Kerala for a memorable and refreshing vacation.

Places of Interest


PERIYAR TIGER RESERVE

Periyar Tiger Reserve is a popular wildlife sanctuary located in the Western Ghats of Kerala. The reserve is known for its rich biodiversity, picturesque landscapes, and a wide range of flora and fauna. It is home to several endangered species, including the Bengal tiger and Indian elephant. Visitors can enjoy trekking, bamboo rafting, and boat safaris while experiencing the beauty of this reserve. The reserve also offers opportunities to learn about the local communities and their traditional lifestyles.

MUNNAR

Munnar is a beautiful hill station located in the Western Ghats of Kerala, known for its tea plantations, lush green hills, and picturesque scenery. It attracts nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and honeymooners from all over the world. The town offers various activities like trekking, camping, and paragliding for adventure lovers. The tea museum, Mattupetty Dam, and the Kundala Lake are some of the popular tourist spots in Munnar. The hill station is also famous for its exotic flora and fauna, including the Neelakurinji flower that blooms once in 12 years.

CHERUTHONI DAM

Cheruthoni Dam is a popular tourist destination located in Idukki district of Kerala. The dam is built on the Cheruthoni River, which is a tributary of the Periyar River. It is one of the largest dams in Kerala and a major source of hydroelectric power. The dam offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys, making it a popular spot for photography and sightseeing. Visitors can also enjoy boating in the reservoir and witness the impressive engineering feat of the dam. The area around the dam is surrounded by lush green forests, providing a serene and tranquil atmosphere for visitors to relax and unwind.
