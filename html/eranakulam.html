Eranakulam


Eranakulam, also known as Kochi, is a coastal city located in the state of Kerala, India. It is a popular tourist destination and one of the major commercial hubs of Kerala. Eranakulam is known for its rich history, diverse culture, and beautiful beaches. The city has a long and interesting history, having been ruled by several dynasties including the Portuguese, Dutch, and British. As a result, Eranakulam has a unique blend of European and Indian architecture, with attractions such as the Dutch Palace, St. Francis Church, and the Jewish Synagogue. The city is also home to the famous Chinese fishing nets, which have become a popular tourist attraction.


Eranakulam is a melting pot of cultures and traditions, with a vibrant food scene that includes local delicacies such as seafood, appam, and biryani. The city is also known for its annual Cochin Carnival, which is held during the last week of December and features parades, music, and other festivities. One of the major attractions in Eranakulam is its beautiful beaches, such as Cherai Beach, Fort Kochi Beach, and Marine Drive. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and water sports at these picturesque beaches. Eranakulam is also a gateway to the backwaters of Kerala, with boat rides available from Marine Drive to nearby destinations such as Kumarakom and Alleppey. Overall, Eranakulam offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

Places of Interest


EDAPALLY ST. GEORGE FORANE CHURCH

Edapally St. George Forane Church is a popular tourist attraction located in the Eranakulam district of Kerala. This historic church, also known as Edapally Palli, is dedicated to St. George and is known for its beautiful architecture and serene ambiance. The church features intricate carvings and decorations, as well as stained-glass windows that add to its charm. Visitors can attend religious services, admire the church's impressive architecture, and take part in various cultural events and festivals that take place throughout the year.

BOLGATTY PALACE

Bolgatty Palace is a beautiful island palace in Kochi, Kerala, which has been converted into a heritage hotel. Built by the Dutch in 1744, the palace is a beautiful blend of Dutch and Kerala architecture. The palace has a beautiful garden, golf course, and swimming pool, making it a perfect place to relax and rejuvenate. Visitors can also enjoy a boat ride to the palace and explore its history and architecture. The palace offers stunning views of the backwaters and is an ideal location for a romantic getaway or a family vacation.

WONDERLA

Wonderla is a popular amusement park located in Kochi, Kerala. Spread over 82 acres of land, it offers over 50 thrilling rides and attractions, including high-speed roller coasters, water slides, and musical fountains. The park also has a dedicated area for children's rides and activities. In addition to the rides, Wonderla also has a variety of food and beverage outlets, souvenir shops, and facilities for corporate events and parties. With its fun and exciting offerings, Wonderla is a popular destination for both locals and tourists alike, and is a must-visit attraction for families and adventure-seekers visiting Kochi.

MANGALAVANAM BIRD SANCTUARY

Mangalavanam Bird Sanctuary is a serene birdwatching spot located in the heart of Kochi. Spread over an area of just 2.74 hectares, it is home to a variety of bird species including the Little Cormorant, Black-crowned Night Heron, and Indian Shag. The sanctuary also has a small freshwater pond, which attracts various migratory birds during the winter months. Visitors can explore the sanctuary on a guided tour and learn more about the fascinating avian life. It's a must-visit spot for nature and bird lovers visiting Kochi.

ERANAKULAM SHIVA TEMPLE

Eranakulam Shiva Temple, also known as Ernakulathappan Temple, is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva located in the heart of Eranakulam city in Kerala. The temple is believed to have been built during the reign of the Maharaja of Cochin, and features exquisite architecture and intricate carvings. It is a popular tourist destination and attracts a large number of devotees during the annual Shivaratri festival. The temple is also known for its famous elephant procession, which takes place during the temple's annual festival, attracting a large number of visitors.

HILL PALACE

Hill Palace, located in Tripunithura, is one of the largest archaeological museums in Kerala. Built in the 19th century by the Kochi royal family, it was their residence until 1950. Now a museum, Hill Palace is famous for its stunning architecture, rich history, and beautiful gardens. Visitors can explore the palace, which includes a heritage museum, archaeological museum, and a deer park. The museum exhibits various artifacts such as royal portraits, sculptures, manuscripts, and antique furniture. The palace grounds also have a beautiful butterfly park and an ethno-medical garden. Hill Palace is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts and anyone who appreciates beauty and culture.

FORT KOCHI

Fort Kochi, situated in the city of Kochi, is a popular tourist destination known for its rich cultural heritage and historic landmarks. The area is famous for its colonial architecture, including the St. Francis Church and the iconic Chinese fishing nets. Visitors can explore the quaint streets, browse local markets for souvenirs, and sample traditional Kerala cuisine. Fort Kochi is also home to numerous art galleries and museums showcasing the region's history and culture. A visit to this charming neighborhood is a must for anyone interested in experiencing the unique blend of colonial and local culture that defines Kochi.

BHOOTHATHAANKETTU

Bhoothathankettu is a beautiful dam and popular tourist destination located in the district of Eranakulam, Kerala. It is surrounded by dense forests and beautiful hills, offering a serene and scenic view of nature. The dam is built over the Periyar River and is an ideal spot for picnics and day trips. Visitors can enjoy boat rides in the river and explore the nearby forest trails. The place is also famous for its mythological tales and legend of a ghost who guards the dam at night. Bhoothathankettu is an excellent destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those seeking a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

CHERAI BEACH

Cherai Beach, located in the Eranakulam district of Kerala, is a beautiful destination for tourists seeking sun, sand, and sea. The beach offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea and is popular for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports. The palm-lined shore is also dotted with shacks and eateries serving fresh seafood and local delicacies. The beach is easily accessible by road and is a great place to relax and unwind. Visitors can also take a stroll along the nearby fishing villages and witness the traditional way of life. Cherai Beach is a must-visit destination for those looking to experience the natural beauty and coastal charm of Kerala.
