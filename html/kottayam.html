Kottayam


Kottayam is a city in the Indian state of Kerala. It is located in central Kerala and is also the administrative capital of Kottayam district. Bordered by the lofty and mighty Western Ghats on the east and the Vembanad Lake and paddy fields of Kuttanad on the west, Kottayam is a land of unique characteristics. Panoramic backwater stretches, lush paddy fields, highlands, hills and hillocks, extensive rubber plantations, places associated with many legends and a totally literate people have given Kottayam District the enviable title: The land of letters, legends, latex and lakes. The city is an important trading center of spices and commercial crops, especially rubber. Most of India's natural rubber originates from the acres of well-kept plantations of Kottayam, also home to the Rubber Board. Kottayam is also called as “Akshara Nagari” which means the “city of letters” considering its contribution to print media and literature.


Kottayam Town is the first town in India to have achieved 100% literacy (a remarkable feat achieved as early as in 1989). English education in South India did actually start at the Old Seminary here at Kottayam in 1813. The first printing press in Kerala was established (CMS Press) here in 1821 by Rev.Benjamin Baily. The first college in the State (CMS College) was also started here at Kottayam in 1840. Maiden printed Malayalam-English and English-Malayalam Dictionaries were published from Kottayam in 1846 and 1847 respectively. The first and only Cooperative Society of writers, authors and publishers (SPCS), for publishing books and periodicals was set up here in 1945. Kottayam is the hometown of a vast number of books and periodicals and is the center of publishing business in the State. Kottayam is the first town in India selected by the Ministry of Environment and Forests, Government of India to be transformed as an Eco City. The Sri K R Narayanan, the former President of India hails from Kottayam District. Kottayam is the ideal take off point for visits to Peermade, Munnar, Thekkady, Ernakulam and the temple city, Madurai. It is also a gateway to the pilgrim centers of Sabarimala, Mannanam, Vaikom, Ettumanoor, Bharananganam, Erumeli, Manarcaud, and so on.

Places of Interest


ILAVEEZHAPOONCHIRA

Ilaveezhapoonchira is a scenic hill station located in the Kottayam district of Kerala, India, known for its breathtaking natural beauty and panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and hills. The name Ilaveezhapoonchira means "valley where leaves don't fall," referring to the fact that the valley is devoid of trees. Visitors can enjoy trekking and hiking to the top of the hill to witness the spectacular sunrise and sunset views. The hill station is also home to several freshwater lakes, making it an ideal spot for a picnic or a relaxing day out in nature. With its stunning natural beauty and peaceful atmosphere, Ilaveezhapoonchira is a must-visit destination for tourists visiting Kerala.

ARUVIKUZHY WATERFALLS

Aruvikkuzhy Waterfalls is a picturesque waterfall located in the Kottayam district of Kerala, India. The waterfall cascades down from a height of 100 feet into a pool of crystal-clear water, surrounded by lush green forests and rocky cliffs. Visitors can enjoy a refreshing dip in the cool waters of the pool or take a leisurely stroll along the scenic walking trail that leads to the waterfall. The waterfall is also surrounded by several small tea plantations, making it an ideal spot for a relaxing day out in nature. With its serene atmosphere and natural beauty, Aruvikkuzhy Waterfalls is a popular tourist attraction in Kerala.

MALARIKKAL VILLAGE TOURISM

Malariakkal Village Tourism is a unique tourism initiative in Kerala, India that offers visitors a glimpse into the daily life and traditions of a rural village. Located in the Kottayam district, the village is known for its scenic beauty, lush greenery, and peaceful atmosphere. Visitors can engage in a variety of activities, including farming, fishing, pottery-making, and traditional cooking, to experience the village's way of life. The village also offers homestays and camping facilities for tourists looking to immerse themselves in the local culture. With its authentic and sustainable approach to tourism, Malariakkal Village Tourism is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a unique and enriching experience.

VEMBANAD LAKE

Vembanad Lake is the largest lake in Kerala, India and is also one of the longest in India. The lake is situated between the districts of Kottayam, Alappuzha, and Ernakulam and is known for its scenic beauty and biodiversity. Visitors can take a leisurely boat ride along the lake to enjoy the picturesque views of the surrounding villages, coconut groves, and paddy fields. The lake is also home to several species of fish, prawns, and crabs, making it a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts. With its tranquil ambiance and natural beauty, Vembanad Lake is a must-visit attraction for tourists visiting Kerala.

VAGAMON

Vagamon is a picturesque hill station located in the Idukki district of Kerala, India. It is known for its lush green landscapes, rolling hills, and tranquil surroundings, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the lush green hills on foot, indulge in paragliding and trekking activities, and experience the serene beauty of the pine forests and meadows. Vagamon is also home to several ancient temples and churches that are steeped in history and culture. With its cool climate, scenic beauty, and cultural attractions, Vagamon is an ideal destination for a relaxing holiday in nature.

ILLICKAL KALLU

Illickal Kallu is a unique rock formation located in Kottayam district of Kerala, India. It is a popular tourist destination due to its natural beauty and scenic surroundings. The rock formation is known for its peculiar shape, which resembles a mushroom, and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Visitors can trek to the top of the rock to enjoy panoramic views of the area, explore the nearby waterfall, and experience the serene beauty of the surrounding forests. The area is also home to several ancient temples and churches, adding to its cultural significance. With its natural beauty and cultural attractions, Illickal Kallu is a must-visit destination in Kerala.
